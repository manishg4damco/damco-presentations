OOB CoverageWhat D365 F&O gives you for free
D365 F&O ships with two procurement-relevant Power BI solutions available through Lifecycle Services (LCS). These are embedded in analytical workspaces and require no additional licensing. They are genuinely useful as a starting point but were designed for single-entity, single-currency scenarios and do not cover GRN, aging, or three-way match.

Limitations
No GRN data. No invoice aging. No 30/60/90 buckets. No PO-to-Invoice variance. Single legal entity only. No multi-currency consolidation. Cannot be customised without modifying the PBIX file manually.

Gap AnalysisWhat OOB does not cover — four custom builds
The four capabilities LensKart needs cannot be delivered by the OOB content described on the previous slide. Each requires custom data transformation and Power BI modelling on top of D365 entities. These are not limitations of D365 — the data exists. The gap is in the analytics layer.
1
GRN Dashboard
Custom Build Required
Goods receipt tracking across vendors and POs. Shows quantity received vs ordered, value variance, receipt-to-invoice linkage, and open GRNs pending invoicing.
Why OOB doesn't cover it
Product receipt (GRN) data lives in PurchPackingSlipHeader/Line entities. The OOB Purchase Spend Analysis content is built entirely from the Invoice side (VendInvoiceTrans). There is no GRN-side report in the standard content.
2
Invoice Aging and Variance
Custom Build Required
Invoice aging buckets (30 / 60 / 90 / 120+ days), PO line price vs invoice line price variance, payment term compliance, and overdue amount by vendor.
Why OOB doesn't cover it
The Vendor Payments workspace shows payment status but does not compute aging buckets with DAX logic, does not compare PO price to invoice price, and has no variance alerting capability.
3
Multi-entity Consolidated View
Custom Build Required
Cross-legal-entity spend analytics with currency consolidation, intercompany transaction elimination, financial dimension filtering, and Row-Level Security by region or business unit.
Why OOB doesn't cover it
All OOB reports operate within a single legal entity. Multi-entity consolidation requires joining ExchangeRateEntity, applying dimension hierarchies, and implementing RLS — none of which exist in the standard content.
4
Contract Compliance Dashboard
Custom Build Required
PO spend against vendor frame agreements, contract utilisation percentage, vendor adherence scoring, and over/under commitment alerts by contract and vendor.
Why OOB doesn't cover it
Purchase Agreement entities (PurchAgreementHeader/Line) are not included in any OOB Power BI content. Linking agreements to actual PO spend and computing utilisation requires a custom data model.

OCR / IDPWhen is document processing actually needed?
The key question: Are all of LensKart's invoices entered in D365 F&O before they reach the finance team?
Scenario A — Invoices are in D365 (most common)
No external OCR required
Invoices are entered directly into D365, or received electronically and imported via EDI or supplier portal
Dashboard reads from VendorInvoiceHeader and VendorInvoiceLine entities in the data lake — structured, clean data
D365 itself has Invoice Capture (built on AI Builder + Azure Form Recognizer) as a native option for PDF invoice intake
No additional tooling. No incremental licensing for document intelligence.
Scenario B — Paper or PDF invoices outside D365
OCR / IDP layer is required
Vendors send paper invoices or unstructured PDFs that are not yet in D365
An extraction layer is needed before data reaches the lake: extract vendor name, invoice number, line items, amounts, dates
D365 Invoice Capture: Microsoft's native solution. Uses AI Builder (Azure Form Recognizer under the hood). Installs from AppSource. Priced at 100 free transactions/month per tenant; additional capacity at $300 per 1,000 transactions/month.
Azure AI Document Intelligence (standalone, formerly Form Recognizer): for high-volume or complex layouts requiring a custom extraction model outside D365's invoice capture pipeline

Why Fabric for LensKart specifically
Azure AD SSO already in place — Fabric integrates natively with the same tenant, no additional identity setup
No separate ETL tool required — Spark notebooks and Dataflows Gen2 run inside the same Fabric workspace

Fabric vs Azure Synapse — when to use which
Fabric: right choice when you are starting new and the primary use case is Power BI reporting. Unified platform, lower operational overhead.
Synapse: better if LensKart already has a Synapse workspace in production with existing pipelines, or if ML workloads beyond Power BI are in scope.

Co-Design7 questions to define your POC together
These are the questions that determine scope, architecture, and OCR need. There are no wrong answers — each answer shapes the POC design. Our goal today is to work through as many of these as possible together.
1
Which markets and legal entities are in scope for Phase 1?
Singapore is our suggested starting point. Understanding which entities to include defines the data volume and whether currency consolidation is needed from day one.
2
Are invoices fully entered in D365 — or do paper or PDF invoices arrive outside the system?
This single answer determines whether OCR / IDP is in scope at all. It has a direct impact on architecture and budget.
3
What reporting cadence is needed — daily refresh, intra-day, or near real-time?
Determines whether Fast Fabric (<15 min) is required or whether a daily scheduled refresh is sufficient. This has a cost implication.
4
Who are the primary consumers of these dashboards?
Procurement managers, finance team, founders, or all three? Audience determines KPI priority, level of aggregation, and how Row-Level Security should be structured.
5
Does LensKart have an existing analytics platform today?
Synapse, Databricks, Tableau, or any other tool already in production? If yes, we integrate rather than replace — the Fabric recommendation may change.
6
What are your top 3 KPIs right now?
From: spend analysis, invoice aging, GRN match rate, contract compliance, vendor performance, PO approval cycle time. These three KPIs define the POC scope.
7
Are purchase agreements and contracts managed inside D365, or do they exist as PDFs or in a separate contract management system?
Determines whether the Contract Compliance dashboard is straightforward (D365 Purchase Agreements module) or requires an IDP extraction step first. Also indicates whether the Contract Compliance build is in scope for the POC or Phase 2.
