## Beat 1 — The Story (Hook) | ~75 sec

> "About two months ago, I was on a call with a lawyer in Australia. He had a product idea. His exact words: 'It's just in my head.'"

> "He described his workflow. His clients' pain. His vision for a SaaS platform. No written brief. No formal budget. No other vendors in the conversation."

> "I did something different. Before the second call, I built a prototype. Not a polished product. A working visual of what he had described. His portal. The compliance workflow. The way lawyers and clients would move through the system. His complete business model on a screen."

> "When he saw it on the second call, something changed. He stopped explaining his idea. He started validating it. He added requirements we had not discussed. And by the end of the call, he said: 'Let's start. Send me what I need to sign.'"

> "He did not say yes because our price was right. He said yes because his vision existed outside his head for the first time. Two conversations. Trust before contract."

**Note:** No slide change during this beat. Title slide stays up. Land on "Trust before contract." Pause fully before moving.

---

## Beat 2 — The Pattern (Pull Back) | ~60 sec

> "That moment does not happen when you lead with HOW."

> "For most of our history, and this is true of most IT services firms, when a client came to us we led with HOW. How we would build it. How many people. How long. How much. We showed capability. We sent estimates. And when we lost work, we told ourselves it was about price."

> "It was not always about price. Sometimes the client already knew HOW it could be built. That was not the question. The question was: does this firm understand WHAT I am actually trying to solve, and WHY it matters?"

> "HOW has become a commodity. Every competent firm can build, estimate, and deliver. What differentiates us is the depth with which we understand the WHAT and the WHY. That is the shift. From pre-sales to sales engineering."

**Note:** Advance to HOW/WHAT contrast slide at "That moment does not happen." HOW, WHAT, WHY are weighted in delivery, not rushed. Pause after "It was not always about price."

---

## Beat 3 — Two More Stories + Outcome Energy | ~50 sec

> "That approach does not always mean building a prototype."

> "Sahand had a secure messaging application. Ten years old. Completely shut down. No vendor would touch it. Damco came back with a full assessment. Every layer of the codebase. Every reason it was broken. Exactly what it would take to bring it back. Then we took complete ownership. The application went live."

> "Aditya Birla New Age Hospitality needed a digital platform for their cafe business across India. Before any contract, Damco built the complete picture. UX, architecture, every integration. Not a proposal. A vision. Damco became their sole technology partner."

> "Tamarind, Visaka, others. Same pattern. Different industries. Same outcome: clients who trusted us before we had finished explaining the price."

**Note:** Advance slides per story (Hoop, then ABNAH). Last line is fast, one breath, move on. The energy here is results, not explanation.

---

## Beat 4 — Tools: AI and Clarity | ~35 sec

> "This approach is supported by how we use technology. We use AI to sharpen our diagnosis, map pain, identify gaps, model solutions faster than before."

> "And once we have shared a proposal with a client, we do not just wait for their response. We use Microsoft Clarity to see how they moved through it. Which sections they came back to. What they skipped. The next conversation starts exactly where their attention is."

> "We listen in the room. And we listen after it."

**Note:** Advance to Clarity slide. Pause between the two final sentences. One breath each.

---

## Beat 5 — The 5 Changes | ~80 sec

> "So what specifically changed? Five things."

> "First: diagnosis before proposal. The first conversation is not a pitch. It is a diagnostic. We understand the real problem before we ever talk about what we can build."

> "Second: trust from day zero. Trust is not earned after the contract is signed. It is built, or lost, in the first conversation. The first call is already delivery."

> "Third: budget is a design constraint, not a barrier. If the pain is real, there is always a tailored solution. Budget tells us how to sequence. It does not tell us whether to engage."

> "Fourth: clarity of fit. At the end of every conversation, the client should feel that Damco is the right partner. Not because we said so. Because the quality of the conversation showed it."

> "Fifth: business value, not technical output. We are not selling code. We are selling what changes for the business when the right solution is in place. Outcomes. Not outputs."

**Note:** Advance to the 5 changes slide. Each point delivered as a personal observation, not a list reading. No need to say "number one, number two" out loud. The rhythm carries it.

---

## Beat 6 — Close | ~55 sec

> "What we are building here is not a sales skill. It is a professional skill. The ability to sit across from someone, understand their real problem with technical and commercial depth, and make it tangible before you propose anything. That is what separates good practitioners from exceptional ones. It is learnable. And it is what we are building."

> "For clients: a partner who understood the real problem before they signed anything."

> "For Damco: work that fits. Scoped cleanly. Trust built before the contract that holds through delivery."

> "For the people in this room: a skill that will define your career far beyond this company and this role."

> "The best work we have won at Damco was won before we wrote a single line of proposal."

**Note:** Advance to the CLIENTS / DAMCO / YOU close slide. Three-layer section: slow down. Final line, no slide advance. Look at the room. Stop.
